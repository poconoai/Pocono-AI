📋 Peer-Reviewed Research Studies

The administrative burden driving demand for the Sentinel Node is documented across leading medical journals, federal surveys, and health policy research. Every statistic Pocono AI publishes is sourced and linkable.

EHR Burden

Sinsky et al. — Annals of Internal Medicine

Physicians spend 5.9 hours per day in the EHR for every 8 hours of patient care — 36 minutes of documentation per hour of face time. The study established the foundational 5.9-hour figure that drives Sentinel Node design targets.

Prior Authorization

AMA 2024 Prior Authorization Survey

Physicians spend an average of 14 hours per week on prior authorization — nearly two full clinical days. 40% report that PA delays cause patients to abandon recommended treatment. Estimated overhead: $68,000 per physician annually.

Legal Admin Burden

Legal Admin Overhead in Independent Firms

Attorneys in independent practices spend 30–40% of billable hours on non-billable administrative tasks including intake processing, document review, and correspondence drafting. The Sentinel Node targets 1.5–2.5 hours of daily reclamation.

Patient Safety

Cognitive Load & Documentation Error

Documentation burden is directly correlated with physician burnout and cognitive load — a patient safety issue, not just an efficiency one. When physicians are rushed through documentation, error rates increase.

AI Liability

Raine v. OpenAI & AI Citation Fabrication

Federal courts have sanctioned attorneys for submitting AI-generated citations to cases that do not exist. Cloud AI models generate from parametric memory and will hallucinate with high confidence. Local RAG eliminates this by grounding every output in retrieved source documents.

Data Sovereignty

BAA vs. Physical Isolation

A Business Associate Agreement is a contractual promise made after your data has already left your building. Physical network isolation eliminates the transmission entirely — the compliance case is architectural, not contractual. HIPAA covered entities cannot waive this distinction.
