12 Simulated Expert Perspectives

We asked 12 expert advisors — spanning Medicine, Law, Patient Advocacy, and AI Architecture — to evaluate the competitive landscape honestly. These represent composite expert positions, not real individuals.

Medicine · Primary Care Physician "I need 5 hours back, not a 12-month IT project"

For solo and small group practices, Nuance DAX and Epic AI are effectively unavailable — the integration timeline alone takes longer than some physicians stay at a practice. Cloud options like Suki and Abridge work, but I send patient conversations to a server I've never seen. With the Sentinel Node, I can start in weeks and my records stay in my building. The prior auth automation alone addresses $68K/year in workflow loss.

✓ Recommends Pocono AI for practices under 10 physicians needing fast deployment without IT infrastructure.
Medicine · Hospital CMO "At health system scale, integration depth matters"

For a 500-bed health system already on Epic, the calculus is different. Nuance DAX and Abridge offer documentation quality and EHR integration that local AI can't yet match at enterprise scale. That said, for clinical data sovereignty during a pilot phase — or for departments where PHI sensitivity is extreme (oncology, psychiatry) — a Sentinel Node deployment is compelling as a parallel track.

→ Recommends cloud for enterprise scale; Pocono AI for high-sensitivity department pilots.
Medicine · Medical Informaticist "Hallucination risk is a patient safety issue, not a feature flag"

The critical differentiator isn't where the data lives — it's what happens when the AI is wrong. Cloud models can generate confident, authoritative hallucinations with no citation trail. The Sentinel Node's RAG architecture forces every output to cite a locally retrieved data chunk. That citation chain is what enables meaningful human-in-the-loop verification. This is a fundamentally safer architecture for clinical use.

✓ Recommends local RAG architecture for any clinical AI touching active patient records.
Law · Healthcare Compliance Attorney "A BAA is a contract, not a firewall"

Under HIPAA's tiered penalty structure, a single breach of a BAA-covered cloud AI can trigger a Tier 3 or Tier 4 violation — up to $1.9M per category. The BAA doesn't prevent the breach; it determines who's liable after. The EAA™ model Pocono AI uses is the only architecture where a covered entity can demonstrate zero transmission of PHI — provable by network logs, not vendor attestation. That's what survives an OCR audit.

✓ Recommends EAA™ deployment model for compliance officers who need audit-proof PHI custody.
Law · Solo Litigation Attorney "ABA Rule 1.6 creates real exposure for cloud legal AI"

Clio Duo and LexisNexis AI are powerful tools, but the moment I put client strategy or privileged communications into a cloud vendor's system, I've created a potential Rule 1.6 problem. The "reasonable efforts" standard is evolving. Mata v. Avianca showed what happens when AI hallucinations reach a federal filing. A local RAG system grounded in my own case files eliminates both the privilege exposure and the hallucination risk from untethered generation.

✓ Recommends Pocono AI for solo litigation practitioners with high-sensitivity matters.
Law · Legal Technology Consultant "Cloud wins on breadth; local wins on defensibility"

LexisNexis AI and Clio offer depth of legal feature coverage that Pocono AI's general-purpose RAG doesn't replicate — full case law search, brief-drafting templates, billing automation. For a high-volume firm doing routine documentation, cloud wins on efficiency. But for a boutique firm doing high-stakes litigation or M&A, the sovereignty model is the only defensible choice when opposing counsel starts asking about data handling.

→ Recommends cloud for volume firms; local sovereignty for boutique high-stakes practices.
Patient Advocacy · Healthcare Consumer Advocate "I want my records analyzed, not monetized"

29% of physicians report a serious adverse event caused by prior authorization delays. 8% report a patient death. The administrative burden on physicians directly harms patients through delayed care. From a patient perspective, a system that reclaims 5 hours of physician time and eliminates cloud exfiltration of medical records is unambiguously better. I don't want my mammogram and mental health history running through a cloud model I didn't consent to.

✓ Strongly endorses local AI for patient privacy; prior auth automation as patient safety intervention.
Patient Advocacy · Disability Rights Specialist "AI denials are already harming vulnerable patients"

82% of appealed prior authorization denials are overturned — meaning most denials are wrong from the start. AI tools that help physicians prepare complete, well-documented prior auth submissions on the first attempt directly reduce the harm disproportionately experienced by patients with chronic conditions and disabilities who can't afford delays. The Sentinel Node's ability to surface complete patient history for prior auth prep is a direct patient safety intervention.

✓ Endorses prior auth automation capability as a high-impact patient safety tool.
Patient Advocacy · Health Data Privacy Researcher "Consent doesn't follow data through cloud infrastructure"

Patients consent to their provider using their records for care. They do not consent to those records training vendor AI models — even when contracts say they don't. The practical reality is that once data enters cloud infrastructure, provenance is difficult to audit. The Sentinel Node is the only architecture where patient data provably stays under the control of the entity the patient consented to. This matters for trust in AI in healthcare broadly.

✓ Recommends local sovereignty as the only consent-compliant architecture for sensitive health data.
AI Architecture · MLOps Engineer "The RAG citation chain is the key patient safety feature"

From a pure ML engineering perspective, the most important architectural decision in clinical AI isn't cloud vs. local — it's grounded vs. ungrounded generation. The Sentinel Node's local RAG forces every output to cite a retrieved document chunk that a clinician can verify. This is architecturally superior to any cloud model generating from parametric memory. The sub-second retrieval on a 76-page clinical document demonstrates the vector DB is properly configured for production use.

✓ Endorses RAG-with-citation as the technically correct architecture for high-stakes inference.
AI Architecture · Security Researcher "Air-gap means air-gap — not network segmentation"

AirgapAI's marketing uses the term "air-gap" but describes what security professionals call network segmentation — the vendor's update mechanism still traverses a controlled path to the deployment. A true air gap means the inference hardware has no outbound network path during operation. The Sentinel Node's update-via-signed-USB model is the only architecture that meets the cryptographic definition. For threat modeling purposes, this eliminates an entire class of supply-chain attack vectors.

✓ Recommends Pocono AI as the only platform that meets the formal security definition of air-gap deployment.
AI Architecture · Open-Source AI Advocate "Open weights mean you can audit what's running on your patient data"

Llama 3/4 and Gemma are Apache 2.0 and MIT licensed — you can read every line of the model architecture. No competitor running proprietary cloud models can say the same. When the model is processing sensitive clinical or legal records, the ability to audit the inference engine is not an academic concern. The Sentinel Node's commitment to open-weight models is both a governance advantage and a hedge against vendor deprecation of proprietary APIs.

✓ Endorses open-weight model deployment for any organization requiring AI auditability.

The Sentinel Node wins on the question that matters most in high-liability environments: Can you prove your patient data never left your building? If your answer needs to survive an OCR audit, a malpractice deposition, or an ABA inquiry, architecture is the only honest answer.
