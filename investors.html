The Market Opportunity

Physicians spend an average of 5.9 hours daily in the EHR — nearly 44% of their working day on clerical tasks rather than patient care. The U.S. has over 1 million active physicians. The legal sector faces an identical burden in discovery and document review.

Cloud AI solves the efficiency problem but creates an existential compliance problem: HIPAA prohibits sending PHI to OpenAI, Google, or Microsoft. Every cloud AI vendor is architecturally incompatible with the healthcare market's core legal requirement.

This is not a niche problem. It is a structural gap that no funded competitor currently fills.

The Pocono AI Solution — Now a HaaS MSP Model

The Sentinel Node™ is a fully localized, air-gapped AI hardware appliance installed inside the client's facility. All vectorization, inference, and logging occur on hardware Pocono AI owns and maintains. Zero data leaves the building. Zero cloud telemetry. Zero HIPAA exposure.

Under the HaaS model, clients pay a low one-time implementation fee ($2,500–$6,000) and a flat monthly subscription ($950–$2,300/month) that covers the hardware lease, software licensing, local RAG architecture, and KYHD SLA. Pocono AI retains hardware ownership — capturing depreciation tax benefits on the balance sheet while building high-valued Monthly Recurring Revenue.

The unit economics are compelling: A single Tier 1 Intro Sentinel Node on a 24-month contract generates $22,800 in guaranteed MRR — vastly outperforming the previous $15,000 one-time CapEx sale over the same period. The HaaS model also dramatically lowers the decision barrier for the target 2–10 provider independent practice ICP, accelerating the path to 100 active nodes.

The hardware custody model creates a defensible moat: competitors cannot replicate the zero-exfiltration guarantee without abandoning their cloud architecture entirely.
