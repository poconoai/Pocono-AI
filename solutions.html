Legal & Compliance Framework

Connecting advanced AI to internal EHR networks (such as Epic or MyChart) requires rigorous adherence to HIPAA compliance. Because our Sentinel Node integrates with your data environment, a Business Associate Agreement (BAA) is required. However, our architecture fundamentally changes the scope of vendor liability.

Defining EAA (Enterprise Autonomy Architecture)

Enterprise Autonomy Architecture (EAA) is a hardware-enforced custody model. Under EAA, the AI infrastructure physically resides entirely within the Covered Entity’s Local Area Network (LAN). EAA ensures that while Pocono AI, LLC provides the hardware, software tuning, and cryptographic updates, the Covered Entity retains absolute physical, network, and cryptographic custody of all PHI. We do not host, process, or transmit your patient data on external cloud servers.

Sample BAA Structural Outline (For Legal Counsel Review)

Our specialized EAA-compliant BAA template is strictly scoped to local infrastructure maintenance, specifically limiting vendor liability to incidental access. Note: Pocono AI, LLC carries comprehensive commercial general liability insurance.

  • Scope of Services: Limited to hardware provisioning, local RAG tuning, and execution of the Outbound-Only Update Protocol.
  • Data Custody Clause: Explicitly states that the Covered Entity retains 100% custody of PHI. Pocono AI, LLC operates zero off-site data processing facilities for client workloads.
  • Incidental Access Limitation: Limits BAA applicability to potential, incidental exposure during authorized on-site hardware maintenance or secured remote-bridge troubleshooting.
  • Audit & Telemetry: Asserts that all inference and vector databases run completely devoid of outbound internet telemetry, architected to eliminate third-party data exfiltration risks.

Note: This structural outline is provided for legal review and integration planning. Formal BAAs are executed post-consultation in conjunction with client legal representation.
