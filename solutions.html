Legal & Compliance Framework

Connecting advanced AI to internal EHR networks requires rigorous adherence to HIPAA compliance. Because our Sentinel Node integrates with your data environment, our architecture fundamentally changes the scope of vendor liability.

Defining EAA (Enterprise Autonomy Architecture)

Enterprise Autonomy Architecture (EAA) is a hardware-enforced custody model. Under EAA, the AI infrastructure physically resides entirely within the Covered Entity's LAN. EAA ensures the Covered Entity retains absolute physical, network, and cryptographic custody of all PHI.

Sample BAA Structural Outline

Our specialized EAA-compliant BAA template is strictly scoped to local infrastructure maintenance, specifically limiting vendor liability to incidental access.

  • Scope of Services: Limited to hardware provisioning, local RAG tuning, and Outbound-Only update protocols.
  • Data Custody Clause: Explicitly states that the Covered Entity retains 100% custody of PHI.
  • Incidental Access Limitation: Limits BAA applicability to authorized on-site maintenance or secured troubleshooting.
  • Audit & Telemetry: Asserts that all inference runs completely devoid of outbound internet telemetry.
