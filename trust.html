The "Invisible Authority" Problem

Public Large Language Models (LLMs) process information within a "black box." When these models are asked to synthesize complex data, they occasionally bridge knowledge gaps with highly plausible, yet entirely fabricated, assertions. In legal and medical environments, submitting an AI-generated hallucination into a court filing or a patient record carries catastrophic liability.

You cannot mitigate this risk with basic software prompting. It requires a structural, mathematical solution applied directly to the database architecture.
