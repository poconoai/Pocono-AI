Marcus or a certified deployment technician conducts a 45-minute remote assessment of your network environment. We need to understand your current EHR system, existing network topology, available rack or floor space, and power configuration. No PHI is shared during this call.

Network topology review (remote, read-only)
Hardware placement recommendation

Your existing IT partner or MSP receives our technical onboarding documentation: VLAN configuration guide, network requirements checklist, power specs, and installation manual. If you don't have an MSP, Pocono AI handles this directly. This call is typically 30 minutes.

VLAN setup guide delivered to IT partner

The Sentinel Node appliance ships from our configuration facility. Sentinel Node ships pre-configured

Phase 2

Document Ingestion & RAG Configuration

Days 15–30
Day 15–16 Document Corpus Planning Your Practice

Your practice identifies the initial document set for ingestion. For medical practices, this is typically the last 12–24 months of patient charts exported as PDFs from your EHR. For legal practices, this is your active matter files. No documents leave your building at any point — all exports go directly onto the local Sentinel Node from within your network.

Document export list agreed (scope, time range, patient cohort)
EHR export format confirmed (PDF standard)
Staging folder created on local network share
Day 17–22 Document Sanitization Pipeline Run Pocono AI

The Sentinel Node's document sanitization pipeline processes your exported PDFs: OCR (Tesseract + NVIDIA vision at 95% confidence threshold), chunking strategy calibration, metadata tagging, and quality verification. Chunking is tuned to your document types — clinical notes require different chunk boundaries than legal discovery documents. This is where the quality of your future queries is determined.

OCR quality report generated (per-document confidence scores)
Chunking strategy calibrated to your document structure
Documents flagged for re-export if below confidence threshold
Day 23–26 Vector Embedding & Qdrant Index Build Pocono AI

Sanitized documents are embedded using nomic-embed-text-v1.5 (768-dimensional, fully local — no API calls) and stored in the Qdrant vector database. Each chunk is tagged with patient/matter ID, source document, page number, timestamp, and encounter date (for medical) or matter number (for legal). This is your provenance layer — every future answer will cite back to this metadata.

Full document corpus vectorized and indexed in Qdrant
Metadata schema confirmed (chunk ID, source, page, date, entity ID)
Index integrity verified — no orphaned chunks
Day 28–29 RAG Pipeline Tuning & Test Queries Joint

Pocono AI runs 20–30 test queries against your actual indexed documents with a clinician or attorney from your practice present (remote session). Retrieval quality is assessed, chunk overlap and similarity thresholds are adjusted, and the Human-in-the-Loop output format is calibrated to match how your team reads and acts on information.

20–30 real test queries run and evaluated by your team
Retrieval quality score documented (target: >90% relevant chunks in top-3)
Output format calibrated (citation style, summary length, confidence display)
Day 30 Phase 2 Checkpoint — RAG Live Joint

The Sentinel Node is now running live queries against your real document corpus. Phase 2 sign-off call confirms retrieval quality, reviews the test query log, and schedules the HITL workflow training session that begins Phase 3.

RAG pipeline confirmed operational on your document corpus
Retrieval quality report delivered to practice
Phase 3 HITL training sessions scheduled
Phase 3

HITL Training & Workflow Calibration

Days 31–45
Day 31–33 Human-in-the-Loop Workflow Design Session Pocono AI

A 90-minute working session with your lead physician or attorney to map the exact HITL workflow for your practice. We identify the 3–5 highest-volume query types your team will use (e.g., "prior auth history for Drug X," "summarize last 3 encounters for this patient"), design the review-and-verify step, and establish the citation verification habit. This is not a demo — it is a calibration session with your actual workflows.

Top 5 use cases documented and configured
HITL review protocol defined for your practice
Citation verification workflow trained
Day 35–38 Staff Training — Clinical / Legal Staff Joint

Two 60-minute remote training sessions for your clinical staff (MAs, nurses, practice managers) or legal staff (paralegals, legal assistants). Training covers: how to submit a query, how to interpret citations, how to verify answers against source documents before acting, and what to do if a citation doesn't match. We train for skepticism, not dependency.

Session 1: Query formation and output interpretation
Session 2: Citation verification and escalation protocol
Quick-reference card printed and posted at workstations
Day 40–43 Supervised Live Use — First Real Queries Your Practice

Your staff begins using the Sentinel Node for real queries with Pocono AI available for same-day support. Any query that returns unexpected results is flagged to the deployment team within 24 hours for RAG tuning. This is your supervised live period — the system is running in production but with a safety net.

First 50 real production queries run by your staff
Any retrieval issues flagged and resolved within 24 hours
Query log reviewed at end of period for tuning opportunities
Day 45 Phase 3 Checkpoint — Workflow Calibrated Joint

Review of first 50 production queries. Retrieval quality assessed, any recurring gaps addressed. Staff confidence survey completed. SLA terms reviewed. Final tuning adjustments made before full independent operation begins in Phase 4.

Production query quality report reviewed
Staff confidence confirmed
Any final RAG tuning completed

What Your Practice Actually Needs to Do

The most common objection we hear is "this sounds complicated." Here is the honest breakdown of your team's time investment:

Practice Manager

~3 hours total. Coordinate the hardware delivery and placement, identify the initial document export scope, and attend the Phase 3 checkpoint calls. No technical knowledge required.

Lead Physician / Attorney

~2 hours total. The HITL workflow design session (90 min) and the Day 60 outcomes review (60 min). Your input here is what makes the system work for your specific practice patterns.

Clinical / Legal Staff

~2 hours training in Phase 3. Two 60-minute sessions. After that, daily use takes seconds per query. Most staff report the system is faster to learn than a new EHR module.

IT Partner / MSP

~4 hours. Brief on Day 4–5, physical installation on Day 10–12. Standard VLAN configuration. No custom code, no integrations, no EHR API work. Follows the provided checklist.
