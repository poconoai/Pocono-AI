The Mata v. Avianca Problem — and the Local Solution

In Mata v. Avianca, a New York federal court sanctioned attorneys $5,000 and ordered a public reprimand for submitting AI-fabricated case citations. A researcher using ChatGPT invented six cases that did not exist — and the attorneys filed them without verification. The court's order made clear: the attorney, not the AI tool, bears the professional responsibility.

Cloud AI models generate confident-sounding citations to cases that do not exist because they are predicting plausible text — not searching an indexed case database. The Sentinel Node uses Retrieval-Augmented Generation: every response cites the specific document chunk it retrieved from your local case files. If the citation does not exist in your indexed documents, the system does not fabricate it. The difference is architectural.
