5. Mandatory Human-in-the-Loop & Audit Log Specification

Pocono AI, LLC provides systems architecture, not medical, clinical, or legal advice. The generative models deployed are probabilistic systems designed strictly as research and discovery tools to augment professional workflows. Because large language models may generate plausible but factually incorrect information (hallucinations) — including from locally retrieved data — all operational use of the Sentinel Node requires a strict Human-in-the-Loop (HITL) protocol mandated at the system architecture level.

The Client explicitly agrees that outputs generated by the Sentinel Node must never be utilized as the sole basis for clinical diagnosis, patient care decisions, or legal strategy without direct human verification against the source data chunks provided by the system. The licensed professional operating the system assumes complete and total liability for the accuracy and appropriateness of their final work product.

Audit Log Architecture: To enforce and support the HITL requirement, the Sentinel Node maintains a local, on-device Audit Log for every query session. This log is stored exclusively on the deployed hardware and is never transmitted to Pocono AI, LLC or any third party.

Each Audit Log entry captures:

  • The verbatim user prompt submitted to the system
  • The specific local document chunks retrieved by the RAG pipeline, with source file references
  • The complete model-generated response
  • A UTC timestamp and local session identifier
  • The quantized model version active at time of query

Minimum log retention is 90 days on local NVMe storage. The Client is responsible for configuring extended retention to meet their applicable regulatory requirements. Audit logs are accessible to the Client's authorized administrators via the local interface at any time.
