Neurodiversity-First Hiring

The hiring page most companies won't publish.

If you're neurodivergent, you've probably spent a hundred interviews trying to figure out whether a "we welcome diverse minds" page actually means anything. Here's what ours means, in detail. Read it, decide if we're a fit, and skip us if we're not. Either way, you save a week of guessing.

The interview is designed to surface what you can do, not whether you perform comfort with a stranger for forty-five minutes. If our process doesn't work for your brain, the process is wrong. Tell us, and we will adjust it.

— Marcus O'Dell, Founder

The Actual Process

How we interview, step by step.

No mystery. No "culture fit" round that's actually vibes-based discrimination. Each step has a specific purpose, a defined time box, and a written deliverable so you know what you're being evaluated on.

  1. Initial conversation — 30 minutes, your format

    async or live · video off allowed · text chat allowed instead

    You pick the format. A live call, a text-based chat, or a written exchange over email. We get curious about your work, what you want next, and what you'd want this role to look like. We ask the same opening question of everyone, and you can see it in advance: "What's a problem you've solved that you're proud of, and what made it hard?"

  2. Take-home work sample — sized to the role

    2–4 hours · paid at your hourly rate · graded on a published rubric

    You receive a real, sanitized version of the kind of work the role does. For engineering, that might be debugging a piece of the audit wrapper. For sales, drafting a discovery call plan for a prospective practice. The rubric is published before you start. You submit, we review, we send written feedback regardless of outcome.

  3. Walkthrough conversation — 60 minutes

    live · agenda sent 24 hrs in advance · breaks welcome

    We talk through your work sample together. You walk us through your reasoning. We ask follow-ups. This is not a "do you have grit?" round — it's a "let's see how you actually think when we're collaborating" round. You can ask us anything. We do not ask brain-teasers, leetcode, or whiteboard-coding-from-memory.

  4. References — from anyone you trust

    2 references · LinkedIn or email contact · we send written questions

    References can be coworkers, peers, mentors — not necessarily managers. We email written questions and accept written replies. We're looking for working-relationship signal, not personality testimony.

  5. Decision — in writing, within 5 business days

    yes · no · "we'd love to keep you in mind for X" — always written

    You hear back from Marcus, in writing, with a real answer. If it's a no, you get the reason. If it's a yes, you get a written offer with the role, the equity terms, the comp, and time to think.

Accommodations Baked In By Default

You don't have to ask. They're already on.

Most companies put accommodations behind a request form, which means you have to disclose to access them, which means many people don't. Ours are the default for everyone. They are not a special arrangement.

Default

Camera-off is fine

For interviews and meetings. We never read into it. Some of us are camera-off most days too.

Default

Questions in advance

For every interview round, you get the agenda and main questions 24 hours ahead. Surprise is not a hiring signal.

Default

Written-first communication

If you process better in writing than on a call, every step has a written-equivalent path. Pick what works.

Default

Take-home is paid

If we're asking for hours of your skilled labor, we pay for it. Pick a rate. We pay it on submission.

Default

Breaks are not weakness

You can take a 5-minute break in any conversation by just saying so. We won't note it. We do it ourselves.

Default

Sensory-considerate workspace

For onsite roles — the East Stroudsburg office is designed for low overhead lighting, quiet zones, headphones welcome, no open-plan stadium seating. Working from home most days is also fine.

Default

Flexible hours

Engineering and ops roles have core overlap hours but otherwise: when you do your best work, you should be working. Early-morning, late-night, split-shift — we don't care, we just need outcomes.

Default

"Direct" is a feature

If you communicate bluntly, that is good here. We don't need you to soften every sentence. We mean what we say too.

How We Actually Work

Our communication norms, on the record.

The thing about culture is that nobody writes it down, and then everybody has a different version of it, and then someone neurodivergent gets penalized for not reading the room nobody documented. Here is the room, documented.

We Do

Write things down

Decisions go in documents. Not in DMs, not in meetings nobody recapped. If it isn't written, it didn't happen.

We Don't

Expect mind-reading

If we want something, we say it directly. Hinting, "you should just know," and passive-aggressive silence are all bugs we treat as bugs.

We Do

Default to async

Slack-style messages, written PRs, recorded looms. Live meetings are for things that genuinely require synchronous discussion. Most things don't.

We Don't

Reward "always on"

Replying at 11pm doesn't earn you points. Burning out doesn't either. Sustainable pace is the strategy.

We Do

Disagree out loud

If you think a decision is wrong, say so. Pushback isn't insubordination. Surface it, we'll discuss, we'll commit.

We Don't

Track keystrokes or webcams

Surveillance tooling has no place here. If we hired you, we trust you. If we don't trust you, that's a different conversation, not a software problem.

Honest disclosure — what we can't promise yet

Where we are, and where we want to be.

We are an early-stage company building this culture in public. The founder lives this directly — chronic-illness management, neurodivergent traits, the whole picture. That gives us conviction; it does not give us a complete program. We don't have a dedicated People team yet (it's in the hiring roadmap). We will hire imperfectly. We will get something wrong and need to fix it.

What we promise: when something is wrong, you can name it without fear, and we will fix it or explain why we can't. That's the floor. We're aiming higher than that, and we'd rather you see the floor than be sold a ceiling.

Sound right? Come work on something that matters.

If you've read this and thought, "yes, that's how it should work," then we want to talk. The open roles are listed on our careers page. The reply lands in Marcus's inbox, not a hiring black box.
