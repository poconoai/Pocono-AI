A physician needs to prescribe Humira for a patient with Crohn's disease. Watch the standard process — then see how the Sentinel Node changes it. Click Run to step through each stage.

1
Physician diagnosis & prescription decision

Physician determines Humira is clinically appropriate for this patient's moderate-to-severe Crohn's disease.

🕑 5 min clinical time
2
MA contacts pharmacy to check formulary

Medical assistant calls pharmacy. On hold. Discovers Humira requires prior authorization from patient's insurer.

🕑 18 min on hold + 8 min call
3
Identify correct insurer PA portal or fax number

MA must locate the correct prior auth pathway for this specific patient's plan. Different plans use different portals, fax numbers, and forms.

🕑 12 min searching
4
Pull patient chart history for clinical justification

MA manually reviews chart to find prior treatment history — what was tried, when, dosages, and outcomes — to justify medical necessity. This is the bottleneck.

🕑 25–45 min chart review
5
Physician reviews and signs clinical justification letter

MA drafts letter. Physician reviews, corrects inaccuracies, and signs. Interrupts patient care to do so.

🕑 15 min physician time
6
Submit PA request via fax or portal

PA request submitted with supporting documentation. Fax confirmation or portal submission receipt saved.

🕑 10 min submission
7
Wait for insurer response

Federal law requires response within 72 hours for urgent, 15 business days for standard. Most responses take 3–10 business days. Patient waits for treatment.

🕑 3–10 business days
8
Initial denial received

Insurer denies — step therapy required. Patient must try and fail a cheaper biologic first. 45% of all PA requests are initially denied.

🕑 Patient waits additional weeks
9
Physician peer-to-peer review request

Physician requests peer-to-peer review with insurer's medical reviewer. Scheduling this call takes 2–5 days. The insurer's reviewer may not be a gastroenterologist.

🕑 2–5 days scheduling + 30 min call
10
Appeal filed with additional documentation

After peer-to-peer denial, formal appeal is filed. Additional chart notes, lab results, and peer-reviewed literature supporting Humira are compiled and submitted.

🕑 3–5 hours staff time
11
Second denial or approval

If approved: prescription proceeds — weeks after the physician's clinical decision. If denied again: external review or patient self-pay options explored.

🕑 Another 15 business days
12
Patient notified & prescription sent

Patient finally notified of approval. Prescription sent to specialty pharmacy. Some patients have already sought care elsewhere or abandoned treatment.

🕑 Total elapsed: 3–8 weeks

The Research Behind These Numbers

AMA 2024

Prior Authorization Physician Survey

3,000 physicians surveyed. 94% report PA delays negatively impact patient outcomes. 40% say patients abandon treatment. Average 14 hours/week spent on PA — across all specialties. Primary care and oncology worst affected.

MGMA 2024

Administrative Cost Analysis

$68,274 average annual per-physician cost of prior authorization processing when fully loaded (staff time, physician time, overhead, and opportunity cost of delayed care). For a 3-physician practice: over $200,000/year in administrative cost generating zero clinical value.

JAMA Internal Medicine 2023

Step Therapy & Patient Harm

Step therapy requirements — forcing patients to "fail first" on cheaper alternatives before getting prescribed treatments — are associated with significant clinical harm, hospitalization, and disease progression in conditions including IBD, rheumatoid arthritis, and severe asthma.

KFF 2024

Denial & Appeal Rates

45% of prior authorization requests receive an initial denial. Of those appealed, 80% are eventually approved — meaning the vast majority of denials are overturned when physicians have time to fight them. Most don't. Complete, accurate documentation on first submission is the highest-value intervention.

What the Sentinel Node Actually Does for Prior Auth

The Sentinel Node does not submit prior auth requests. It does not communicate with insurers. It does not integrate with any insurer portal. What it does — which is where most of the time is lost — is retrieve and format the clinical justification documentation from your local patient records, accurately, in seconds, with citations.
