Legal & Compliance Framework

Connecting advanced AI to internal EHR networks requires rigorous adherence to HIPAA compliance. Because our Sentinel Node integrates with your data environment, our architecture fundamentally changes the scope of vendor liability.

Defining EAA (Enterprise Autonomy Architecture)

Enterprise Autonomy Architecture (EAA) is a hardware-enforced custody model. Under EAA, the AI infrastructure physically resides entirely within the Covered Entity's LAN. EAA ensures the Covered Entity retains absolute physical, network, and cryptographic custody of all PHI.

BAA & Redline Posture

We understand that major medical networks and Am Law 200 firms do not sign vendor BAAs — they issue their own. We are prepared for that. Our deployment team maintains a Redline Playbook: a set of technically grounded insertions that can be applied to any client-issued BAA to accurately reflect the EAA™ air-gap architecture.

  • Data Custody Clause: Inserts language confirming the Covered Entity retains 100% physical and digital custody of all PHI — language no cloud AI vendor can truthfully offer.
  • Incidental Access Limitation: Narrows vendor liability to authorized on-site maintenance only, with no remote access carve-outs.
  • Zero Telemetry Assertion: Technically defines the air-gap such that outbound data transmission by the Sentinel Node is architecturally impossible, not merely contractually prohibited.
  • Audit Trail: Confirms all inference runs are logged locally and available for client audit, with no copy retained by the vendor.
