The Problem

5.9 hrs Daily EHR Burden

Studies show physicians spend an average of 5.9 hours daily in the EHR — nearly 44% of their day on clerical tasks rather than patient care. An estimated 350,000 Americans die each year from preventable medical errors, many linked to documentation failures and inaccessible prior records.

The Solution

up to 5 hrs Reclaimed Daily*

The Sentinel Node automates chart prep, document summarization, and clinical triage locally — giving physicians fast, accurate access to prior documentation without sending a single byte to the cloud.

*Based on early pilot workflows. Human-in-the-Loop protocol required: Licensed professionals verify all cited local data outputs.

Operational Deployment Model

Reduced Exfiltration

Prevents PHI exfiltration by eliminating outbound cloud connections for primary inference workflows.

Auditable Local AI

Turnkey models designed for Human-in-the-Loop workflows. Every output provides cited local data chunks for mandatory verification by the licensed professional.

Air-Gapped Security

Physical isolation of mission-critical weights. Updates delivered via cryptographically signed, SHA-256 verified USB packets only — no automatic network connections.

Managed Sovereignty

You retain custody of hardware, weights, and records. No third-party data access during inference. BAA available for all deployment partners.

Why I Built This

Built from Necessity. Driven by Mission.

I came up as a systems and database administrator — a decade-plus of enterprise MySQL, PostgreSQL, MongoDB, Linux hardening, and large-scale data architecture — with a foundation in Psychology (Master's Degree) that taught me how people actually think and work under pressure. I understood both the human side of high-stakes environments and the technical side of the systems holding their data.

Then I became disabled. For five years, contributing at the level I was built for felt out of reach. Typing on a keyboard was frustrating. Typing on a phone was worse. The executive dysfunction that came with my condition made it hard to articulate what I was thinking, even when the ideas were there. I watched the world move without me.

Voice-to-AI changed that. Speaking into my phone, directing an AI, making minor edits — suddenly I could architect systems from bed. I could build. I could contribute again. That experience isn't a footnote to this company. It is this company.

"AI was my equalizer. It bridged a physical gap I couldn't close on my own. Now I want to bring that same capability to medical practices — so physicians can spend less time buried in documentation and more time with the patients whose lives depend on accurate, accessible records."

I'm also building a companion device — a Mini Sentinel Node for personal use — that listens to what you're trying to say and outputs it articulately for you. Not everyone will want one. But for those of us who struggle to find the words, it matters. I will donate units, free of charge, to anyone who wants one — funded from Pocono AI's after-tax income as we grow. That's not a marketing promise. It's a personal commitment.

The same drive that got me through five years of disability is what's behind the Sentinel Node. I know what it means to need systems that empower you rather than fail you. Physicians lose 350,000 patients a year in part because they can't quickly surface accurate prior documentation. I intend to help fix that.

Technical Brief: Achieving Zero Telemetry

The conversation around AI safety often misses a structural reality: If you want to stop an AI model from "phoning home," you have to engineer an environment where it physically cannot.

At Pocono AI, we enforce strict hardware and network firewalls. A proper sovereign deployment places the AI infrastructure on a physically isolated network — routing traffic in to cordon the system off from outbound access.
