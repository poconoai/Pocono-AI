Why Regulators Ask About AI Systems

OCR Investigations

The HHS Office for Civil Rights has begun asking about AI tool usage in breach investigations. A practice that cannot articulate how their AI system handles PHI — or that discovers it has been transmitting PHI to cloud vendors without a BAA — faces significantly elevated penalty risk.

Malpractice Defense

When AI-generated clinical documentation is involved in a malpractice claim, attorneys will ask how the AI was configured, what data it accessed, and whether it could have introduced errors. A sovereignty attestation establishes your system's architecture as part of your defense record.

Insurance Renewals

Malpractice and cyber liability insurers are beginning to ask about AI tool usage during renewals. Practices using cloud AI without BAAs may face coverage exclusions or premium increases. Documentation of your compliance posture is increasingly expected.

State Attorney General

Several states (CA, NY, TX) have passed or are passing state-level healthcare AI disclosure requirements. Being able to immediately produce a clear, accurate description of your AI system's data handling is not optional — it is a compliance baseline.

The Cleanest Attestation Is No Transmission

A Sentinel Node attestation is one paragraph: the system runs locally, processes no external API calls, and PHI does not leave the building. No BAA drama. No cloud vendor to investigate. Just hardware you own.
